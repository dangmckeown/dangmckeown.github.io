View the latest Fantasy Draft League standings (Heroku)

Background

I'm part of a 'Draft League', using the scores from fantasy.premierleague.com. Now there's an automatic-updating league table to show how badly taking Leonardo Ulloa over Jamie Vardy is hurting me, among other things.

The app is built in PHP, using data from the fantasy.premierleague.com API

The draft

On the day of the Community Shield match between Arsenal and Chelsea, we gathered in the Porterhouse near covent garden, clutching handfuls of notes, ready for our inaugural Fantasy League draft.

The rules

  • Take it in turns to pick.
  • The last manager to pick in round one gets first pick in round two and the picking order is reversed
  • Pick a squad of 15: two goalies, five defenders, five midfielders, three attackers
  • The real game-changer: pick no more than one player per Premier League team
  • Don't use any tech - bring your research with you

The first round of the draft went as might be expected: Rooney and Aguero, top scorers from last year, were quickly off the table. The second round was where the 'one player from each team' rule came into play. Pete's decision to hit West Brom early and take Saido Berahino led to a run on West Brom assets. Then, with all of Arsenal, Chelsea, Man City and Liverpool to choose from, I snapped up Liam Cresswell from West Ham.

Some observations

  • Unless there's someone you really like, don't get into any of the big teams early. Even after the other four managers had picked a Chelsea player each, there were still about eight left I would have been reasonably happy to take.
  • That said, Man Utd genuinely only had two players I wanted. I got Rooney, but if I couldn't have taken him or Mata, I doubt I would have bothered getting a Man U player at all.
  • At the time, getting the star players from smaller teams seemed like the be-all and end-all. When Gylfi Sigurdsson went off the table, it was like a knife through my heart.
  • Beware the availability heuristic: with a straight choice between Ulloa and Vardy from Leicester (level on points from last season, no one else was interested in Leicester strikers), I went with big Ulloa because I had a better idea what he looked like and talked myself into imagining Leicester booting it up the pitch to him.
